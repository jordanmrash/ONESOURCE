What is a workflow?

A workflow is a “wrapper” around a process that helps you keep track of the tasks, documents, people, checklists, notes, research, and other components needed to accomplish the overall process goal.


Terminology

These terms are commonly used in ONESOURCE WorkFlow Manager.

Checklist – A checklist is a list of items to be considered or consulted. Users indicate that they have done so by selecting Yes, No, or Not Applicable for each item on the list. Checklists can be associated with a specific task or with all tasks in a workflow.

DataFlow – DataFlow is used to collect information from data providers in order to complete a workflow. A DataFlow request is created by a ONESOURCE WorkFlow Manager user and sent by e-mail to data providers. DataFlow requests and their associated files can be tracked within or across workflows.

Document – An electronic file or hard copy information that can be added to (uploaded) to the FileRoom portion of the WorkFlow Manager suite. Documents can be filed in general or tied to a specific workflow.

Entity – Business units, companies, branches, divisions, individuals, trusts, subsidiaries, and unrelated third parties or investments for which data is maintained.

Event tasks – Event tasks are statutory tasks such as filing a tax return or submitting a payment. An event task is mandatory, and is imposed by the jurisdiction’s Authority for that topic. For example, the Securities & Exchange Committee (SEC) requires certain companies to file a 10K by the third month following their financial year end. ONESOURCE Calendar includes content for event tasks across a wide range of statutory tasks.

Instantiation – The process by which a workflow template is put into process. During instantiation, the workflow template is assigned to a specific Entity, Client, Year, Jurisdiction, or other key parameter. Once instantiated, you can begin your work within a workflow.

Notes – Within a workflow, different users can leave notes on various issues or use e-mail notification to alert others of the note. Notes can be used to track open items during review process.

ONESOURCE platform – The environment that you see when you first log on to ONESOURCE is called the ONESOURCE platform. From the platform, you can access all ONESOURCE products, review key metrics, and work on your tasks by using the My Work application on the platform.

Template Library – A workflow can come pre-loaded with documents from the Template Library. When administrators set up a workflow, they have the ability to select which documents automatically come with that workflow.

Workflow – A workflow is a “wrapper” around a process that helps you keep track of the tasks, documents, people, checklists, notes, research, and other components needed to accomplish the overall process goal.

Work tasks – Work tasks are non-Calendar events that contribute to complying with statutory requirements or that satisfy internal processes. For example, getting a check issued by your internal Finance department or getting trial balance data from a subsidiary’s comptroller are considered work tasks.


Workflow template – A predefined workflow that is not yet assigned to a specific Entity, Client, Year, Period, Jurisdiction or other key parameter. It is simply the workflow definition such as list of tasks, events, checklists, and other workflow components.

About rules-based workflows

Rules-based workflows are designed to make your work more efficient. Rules are conditional statements that are either user-defined or contained in best practice workflows. In ONESOURCE Workflow Manager, rules are built in to each workflow, and can change what and when tasks are due depending on how you answer questions or change task status. Based on facts gathered, rules can change who sees a task, when that task is due, or even which tasks should be completed.

Your tasks appear automatically on your My Work page when you first log in to ONESOURCE. Keep in mind that only tasks assigned to you or your role will appear on your My Work page.

For example, if you are a tax preparer, you will see tasks assigned to your role, such as entering tax data or gathering filing information. You will not see tasks assigned to other roles, such as tax reviewer.

Remember that while serial/parallel tasks are seen only by the one person that task is assigned to,

everyone with the same role sees a rules-based workflow task.

NOTE: Your administrator creates the workflow templates (serial, parallel, or rules-based)

complete with all settings such as escalation levels, assignments, and due dates.

Components of a rules-based workflow

Each rules-based workflow consists of the following components:


  • Roles – Your administrator establishes roles and the number of escalation days

    required for each workflow task category, such as tax payment or tax filing.

  • Tasks/events – The tasks and events appear during the workflow. Each task and event has its own set of fields, rules, and instructions.

  • Assignments – The assignments are based on who gets to see the task/event, and at what point in time.

    Tasks in a rules-based workflow

    Tasks in a rules-based workflow add another dimension of knowledge that allow ONESOURCE WorkFlow Manager to process tasks in an intelligent fashion. Each rules-based workflow task can ask a user for more facts, such as whether the company is active or not, has a short/long period of account, or is a large or small company.

    Based on answers given by the user working on this task, other tasks are launched or other

    questions asked so that only the relevant tasks appear across users

    Event tasks can be listed as tasks within a rules-based workflow process. Doing so automatically draws from Calendar content and populates Calendar Scheduled Event with statutory due dates while launching event task in My Work as needed to the assigned roles.

    NOTE: For serial and parallel workflows, event tasks appear only on the Events grid. You must populate the Calendar Scheduled Event area manually within the Calendar module. For more information on integration with Calendar for serial and parallel workflows, please refer to the


    “Scheduling Calendar Events” section of the ONESOURCE Calendar user guide.

    Comparing Serial/Parallel workflows to Rules-based workflows

    Serial/parallel and rules-based workflows are similar, but do have several differences as explained

    in the following section.

    Task Assignment





  • Task Types




  • Serial or parallel workflows: The administrator assigns a task to just one person.

    Rules-based workflows: The administrator assigns a task to a group of people who share a similar role or responsibilities. Everyone with that role sees the urgency of that task. You do not see tasks that are not relevant to your role.

    Instead, you see only what is relevant to your role at a particular point in time.

    Example: In a serial/parallel workflow, suppose that your administrator assigns Joe to pay a tax for John Doe. Joe will then be the only one who will see this assignment. In a rules-based workflow, your administrator can assign the task to the role of Finance so that all accountants with the Finance role see the task that needs to be done.


    Serial or parallel workflows: Each task is only a work task. Tasks are not linked to Calendar events.

    Rules-based workflows: Tasks can be work tasks or event tasks that are integrated with Calendar events. An event task is a scheduled event from Calendar that also appears in the workflow. In My Work, the event task follows escalation rules and adjusts for your non-working days. In Calendar, the same event will stay true to its statutory deadline, adjusting only for jurisdictional holidays and weekends as needed.

    Example: The workflow on how best to file an Indiana Income Tax return comes from Thomson Reuters pre-configured with proper interview (proforma) questions and the Event Templates needed to then schedule Calendar events as required.

    Process Completion

  • Serial or parallel workflows: You work through a predetermined list of tasks to complete a process.

  • Rules-based workflows: You work through a wizard that prompts you with each step to accomplish a process. This offers you the ability to ask questions and gather facts when a process is started. For every task related to that process, you are prompted with questions related to that task. Answers are handled by conditional statements (pre-set rules) that then drive the process and tasks in the appropriate direction. The benefit is greater accuracy and efficiency.

    Example: Suppose that the workflow for the Indiana Sales Tax process includes a question regarding sales amount. The workflow wizard will ask you if sales for


    this month are greater than $15,000. If you answer “Yes”, then the due date is scheduled for 20th of the month. If you answer “No”, then return is not due until the end of the month and the due date is scheduled for the last day of the month.

    Status and Reporting





  • Visibility




  • Serial or parallel workflows: In order to gain additional information about a task, you view reports or gadgets, then filter or search by people or dates.

    Rules-based workflows: When tasks reach a critical state, relevant people will see task in their My Work application. Rules-based workflows offer additional visibility into the process, including color coding according to urgency and an e-mailed digest of reports and gadgets

    Example: When a tax preparer does not pay a tax within X number of days prior to the due date, his boss can view that task in My Work as escalated according to the color that appears with the task.


    Serial or parallel workflows: Routing of tasks by users is the only way to enforce that tasks are done in a certain order.

    Rules-based workflows: Visibility of a task in an individual’s My Work list can be controlled by roles, escalations, and rules which are conditional statements. Tasks work off the answers to questions and facts already stored

    in ONESOURCE applications. For example, Tax Year End is stored in Entity Manager, but can be used in a rules-based workflow This means that the possibilities and permutations for enforcing task completion and custom solutions are endless.

    Example 1: As soon as Mary completes Task 2, Task 5 is automatically routed to John in Legal, but only if John answers that this is the last year for this taxable entity. Otherwise, Task 5 is automatically routed to Susan in Accounts Payable, prompting her to write the check for the proper amount.

    Example 2: Susan must answer questions to determine if the check will be submitted on paper or electronically, if there is a dollar limit on the check amount, as well as who is authorized to sign the check.

  • Serial or parallel workflows: Using the serial and parallel workflows often

    results in a more complex end user experience.
